Lorax Loots Onceler's Green Grant: A Dramatic Environmental Heist

In a scandalous turn beneath Truffula trees, The Lorax has seized funds intended to ease The struggles of waterways, wetlands, and lakes, Leaving the Once-ler astonished, betrayed in his stakes.

Late yesterday evening, beneath sunset's glow, In a spectacle shocking as fresh-fallen snow, The Lorax—long champion of fauna and trees— Made off with a fortune, with questionable ease.

“I speak for the trees!” he had always declared, Yet his motives now murky, intentions impaired. The Once-ler had vowed he would right past mistakes, Fund studies of rivers, protect ponds and lakes.

But moments before he could make this amend, The Lorax swooped in—this once faithful friend— And snatched every dollar, from pennies to pounds, And vanished quite swiftly without any sounds.

"These dollars were destined for fishes and frogs, To cleanse murky rivers, lift fogs over bogs!" The Once-ler lamented, in deepening gloom, Facing protests from creatures demanding his doom.

The Swomee-Swans sobbed, and the Bar-ba-loots wailed, The Humming-Fish hummed low, their trust badly failed. And residents whispered, in shock and despair, "Could it be that the Lorax no longer cares?"

Environmentalists gathered in grief and in fright, Bewildered, betrayed on this scandalous night. Now rumors swirl fiercely around Brown and beyond: Was the Lorax corrupted? By greed correspond?

A hearing is scheduled beneath Grickle-grass, As crowds will assemble to witness the clash. For justice, for answers, the public demands, While Lorax stays hidden in shadowed woodlands.
