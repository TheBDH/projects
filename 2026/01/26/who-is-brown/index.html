Who is Brown?


The Herald analyzed 20 years of demographic data to understand how the Brown community has evolved over time.


Reporting by Caleb Ellenberg

Data analysis by Dayna Phan Rahul Sameer Joy Zou

Web design by Dhruv Jain Caleb Ellenberg

↓

In fall 2024, 16,916 people made up the Brown community. From graduate students to professors to maintenance staff, every one of those people is part of Brown’s identity. The Herald’s data desk investigated demographic trends over the span of 20 years from 2004 to 2024. This article explores who Brown is and was, starting from the biggest picture and steadily zooming in.

The largest category is Brown’s 11,205 students, which includes undergraduate, graduate, and medical students. The 1,740 faculty members include both research and instructional staff. Brown employs 3,971 staff, including librarians, lawyers and dining staff.

Brown has long stood out among Ivy League universities for its focus on undergraduate education. Brown considers itself a “university-college,” a term that former President Henry Wriston used to describe a liberal arts perspective towards education and research. That focus has shifted in recent decades as Brown has worked to position itself as a premier research institution. Graduate student enrollment has more than doubled since 2004, and medical student enrollment increased by 81%. Undergraduate enrollment has also increased, but by a more modest 25%.

The faculty category includes all employees in instructional or research positions. Faculty who both teach and research are categorized as instructional. Matching the growth in the student body, the total faculty count has grown by 58% since 2004. That increase was especially concentrated in research-only faculty, which has nearly doubled since 2004. This growth is likely to slow following Brown’s decision to limit faculty hiring last winter amid financial struggles.

In 2024, Brown had 880 “regular faculty,” which includes professors and distinguished senior lecturers. In 2015, 66% of “regular faculty” were men and 34% were women, a gap that has somewhat narrowed in recent years to 60% men, 40% women in 2024.

Brown’s 3,971 staff employees make the university work. Dining staff run the Blue Room and 7 other dining locations. Archivists conserve and catalog historical documents at the John Hay Library. Career advisors help students determine their path after graduation. Brown splits staff into 13 broad categories, the largest of which are “Computer, Engineering, Science,” “Business and Financial Operations” and “Service.”

Together, these thousands of students, faculty and staff make Brown who it is.
